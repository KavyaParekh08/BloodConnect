Donor Guidelines

Eligibility Criteria

To ensure the safety of both donors and recipients, potential donors must meet certain criteria. These include being in good health, weighing at least 110 pounds, and being at least 17 years old. Additional criteria may apply based on travel history, medical conditions, and medications. A detailed questionnaire is available to help potential donors assess their eligibility.

Donation Process

The blood donation process typically takes about an hour from start to finish. It begins with registration and a health screening, followed by the actual blood donation, which usually takes about 10 minutes. After donating, donors are monitored briefly and provided with refreshments to help them recover. The entire process is designed to be safe, comfortable, and efficient.

Preparation Tips

Proper preparation can enhance the donation experience. Donors are advised to eat a healthy meal and drink plenty of fluids before donating. It's also recommended to avoid fatty foods and alcohol. Bringing a list of medications and any relevant medical history can help streamline the screening process. Wearing comfortable clothing with sleeves that can be easily raised is also advisable.

Post-Donation Care

After donating blood, it's important to take care of yourself to ensure a quick recovery. Donors should continue to drink fluids and avoid strenuous activities for the rest of the day. If any discomfort or dizziness occurs, it's recommended to lie down with feet elevated. Donors are also advised to avoid smoking and alcohol for several hours after donating. A follow-up information sheet is provided with detailed post-donation care instructions.
